# Acknowledgments

This book would not exist in its present form without a particular spark.

I want to explicitly acknowledge **Wu Ji** and the paper **“Trust Over Fear: How Motivation Framing in System Prompts Affects AI Agent Debugging Depth”**<sup><a href="/appendices/sources#source-trust-over-fear-paper">[1]</a></sup> together with the associated NoPUA project<sup><a href="/appendices/sources#source-nopua">[2]</a></sup>.

Reading that paper was the moment that pushed the underlying question from vague intuition into deliberate reflection. It did not provide every answer in this book, and this book does not reduce to that paper. But it played a real role in helping the central problem become visible:

**how we frame work for AI changes the depth and quality of what comes back.**

For that reason, this book owes a clear debt of gratitude to the author and that line of work.

I also want to acknowledge the quieter source of the book: repeated friction in real conversations with AI. The irritation of shallow outputs, premature closure, and false completeness turned out to be productive. Without that friction, there would have been no reason to write this guide.
