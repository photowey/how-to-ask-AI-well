# 20. How Results Will Be Judged

If labs and case studies only compare outputs aesthetically—what “feels” better—the whole section collapses into opinion. The evaluation criteria must be explicit. When you run a hands-on exercise yourself, score the outputs on the same dimensions so tone does not substitute for evidence.

## Primary criteria

The labs will judge results across dimensions such as:

1. **Depth**
   Did the answer go beyond the visible task surface?

2. **Completeness**
   Did it close the work within the stated scope, or stop too early?

3. **Verification quality**
   Did it provide evidence, validation steps, or merely confidence?

4. **Hidden issue discovery**
   Did it find linked or latent problems not named in the original ask?

5. **Design quality**
   Where design is involved, did it reduce future change cost in a meaningful way?

## Secondary criteria

Depending on the case, additional criteria may include:

- clarity of explanation
- transparency of assumptions
- ability to separate evidence from inference
- discipline around non-goals and boundaries

## What these criteria guard against

They are not here to fake precision. They are here to block vague praise. The labs and case studies should show *why* one prompt structure produced a different result—not only *that* it did.
